APOLLO ISSUES DISCOVERED

Note that when Apollo's bounding box (not 3D model) intersects with any obstacle's bounding box, a collision occurs.
The bounding box is a bit larger than Apollo's 3D model showing in the video (i.e. Apollo's website-based human machine interface).
As a result, collision may trigger even when there is slight gap between Apollo's 3D model and obstacle's bounding box.

1) Soft Braking in Emergencies

Apollo's braking power is capped at 50% by default configuration. In some emergency situations, e.g., where a stopped NPC vehicle suddenly crosses, the ego vehicle brakes too gently and collides with the NPC. Those collisions could have been avoided with full braking power.

Video description

The ego vehicle must brake immediately when the NPC vehicles start their motion at around 2s. However, the ego brakes too softly capped at 50% of the maximal braking force. Thus, a collision occurs at (5s). Had the ego vehicle braked with 100% braking force, it could have stopped where the planned trajectory ends (5s) and avoided the collision.

2) Wrong Overtaking Actions

Apollo often fails to overtake and collides with slow NPC vehicles. It is safe for the ego vehicle to yield instead of trying to overtake even though it may have the right of way.

Video description

Upon detecting the crossing NPC vehicles from left (3s), Apollo decides to "overtake" the crossing vehicles as they are moving relatively slow. At 6s, the ego vehicle nearly bypasses the first NPC10000 which is already quite dangerous. Soon the ego vehicle decides to yield to the second NPC10003 (7s). However, the speed (36km/h) is too fast to avoid the collision at (8s). The collision could have be avoided had the ego braked early (3s) and yielded to the two NPC vehicles.

3) Wrong Trajectory (a)

In some situations, Apollo's planning module fails to produce an correct trajectory, resulting in collision with obstacles or getting stuck at sharp turns.

Video description

When Apollo decides to yield to the crossing NPC vehicles at (2s), it can still manage to stop before NPC10003's driving lane with full braking power. However, instead of yielding to the nearest NPC10003, it yields to NPC10002. This decision results in Apollo's trajectory cutting into NPC10003's driving path, which eventually leads to the collision at 4s.

3) Wrong Trajectory (b)

In some situations, Apollo's planning module fails to produce an correct trajectory, resulting in collision with obstacles or getting stuck at sharp turns.

Video description

The ego vehicle has the prior knowledge from the HD map files about the junction and the lanes inside. It should know that the turn in the junction is relatively sharp. As a result, the planning and control module should work together to handle the sharp turn with more turning and slower speed, instead of getting stuck in the junction blocking the traffic (20s).
Moreover, Apollo needs to detect when it is stuck and performs a fallback solution to get out of the situation, such as temporarily allowing itself to drive on top of the lane boundaries when it is safe (e.g. no other obstacles around).

4) Deadlock

Apollo has not implemented deadlock detection and resolution strategies. When Apollo gets trapped in a deadlock with nearby NPC vehicles inside a junction, it cannot reroute a fallback path, thus jamming the traffic forever.

Video description

After entering the junction (7s), Apollo maintains its speed as the predicted path for NPC10005 is also going straight. At 9s Apollo detects that NPC10005 is turning left, it brakes immediately to avoid collision. The NPC10005, controlled by the LGSVL simulator, also decelerates timely (10s). After both of them come to a complete stop, each blocks part of the other's driving lane (11s). Since neither has implemented the deadlock recognition and resolution strategies, e.g. reversing or nudging from the side, they get trapped in the deadlock and jam the traffic forever.

5) Wrong Prediction

In a few cases, Apollo's prediction module produces incorrect path predictions of crossing NPC vehicles nearby, greatly affecting planning module's decision and eventually leading to collisions.

Video description

When Apollo first detects the crossing NPC10003 before entering the junction (3s), the predicted trajectory for NPC 10003 is correct, which merges into Apollo's routed path. Apollo decides to yield to it (4s). After Apollo enters the junction, it predicts the NPC 10003 to nudge Apollo via changing to the next lane on the right side (5s). This mis-prediction makes Apollo maintain its speed and eventually leads to the collision at (7s).

6) Planning Lag

In four of the failed test cases, Apollo experiences short period of planning lag, where planning module takes much longer to calculate the next trajectory. Sometimes Apollo collides with NPC vehicles while following the old trajectory.

Video description

When Apollo approaches the junction, it first stops and yields to the crossing NPC vehicles before (6s). After the faster NPC10001 bypasses, Apollo decides to overtake the slower NPC10005 (7s). However, after the "overtaking" trajectory is calculated and passed to the control module, the planning module gets stuck and stops publishing new trajectories (the lag being highlighted in red at Dreamview's "Module Delay" monitor) (8s). Furthermore, the control module, without realizing the abnormal delay from the planning module, follows the last trajectory it receives blindly and eventually collides with NPC10005 (9s).

7) Traffic Accidents

Sometimes there are traffic accidents in the junction blocking the ego's way before it reaches. Apollo has not implemented traffic accident recognition and response strategies, and thus stops on the blocked route instead of re-routing a new path or nudging from the side.

Video description

Apollo notices that two vehicles collide in the junction and block its route before it enters the junction. (5s). Instead of re-route another valid path, such as driving straight across or nudging from the side, Apollo follows its original route then stops inside the junction forever (20s).

8) Wrong Switch from Yielding to Overtaking

In some cases, Apollo first yield then overtake the crossing NPC vehicles before they pass the collision region. This wrong switch of decision often results in collisions.

Video description

At 12s, when the predicted trajectory of NPC10002 first intersects the right boundary of Apollo's junction lane, Apollo first yields to NPC10002. Soon Apollo notices that the NPC10002 is relatively slow (1.2m/s) and decides to overtake (13s), which results in the collision later at 21s. Had Apollo maintained its yielding decision, the collision could have been avoided.

9) Prediction Error

In this particular failed test case, Apollo's prediction module goes completely offline for a few seconds, which directly brings the planning module offline at the same time. Apollo's control module follows the old trajectory blindly and collides with crossing NPC vehicles.

Video description

At 2s when Apollo is approaching the junction, the prediction module suddenly goes offline and stops publishing prediction messages. Since the planning module strictly relies on the prediction message, it cannot calculate new trajectories any more. Then the control module does not notice this situation and follows the last planned trajectory it receives blindly, which results in the collision at 10s.

SIMULATOR ISSUES DISCOVERED

The LGSVL simulator is still under rapid development and issues occur sometimes. Those issues causing collisions between NPC vehicles and Apollo are also recorded and analyzed, but Apollo is not held responsible for the collision.

10) Obstacle Failure

The NPC controllers implemented in LGSVL simulator are relatively naive. When the NPCs gets knocked out of their designed path after a collision, the controllers cannot bring them back on track and often starts to drive randomly (what appears to be reckless driving).

Video description

When Apollo is about to enter the junction at 3s, NPC10005 and NPC10001 collides in the junction. Since the NPC controllers. This and subsequent collision on NPC10005 push the vehicle far from its original path. This seems to confuse NPC10005's controller and later it drivers towards the stopped Apollo and the collision occurs (8s). Although Apollo is involved in the collision, we don't not hold Apollo responsible for this.
